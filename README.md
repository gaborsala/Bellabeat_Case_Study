## Introduction

This case study simulates junior data analyst jobs. I’ll work for
Bellabeat, a high-tech company that makes women’s health goods. Data
analysis involves asking, preparing, processing, analyzing, sharing, and
acting to solve crucial business issues. The Case Study Roadmap
tables—including guiding questions and critical tasks—will keep me on
track. This class produces a portfolio-ready case study. My case study
will then show potential employers your expertise and talents.

Scenario I’m a junior data analyst on Bellabeat’s marketing analyst
team, which makes high-tech women’s health products. Bellabeat is a
small but successful smart gadget startup. Bellabeat co-founder and
Chief Creative Officer Urška Sršen thinks examining smart device fitness
data might help the company develop. I’ll examine smart device data for
one of Bellabeat’s products to learn how consumers use smart devices. My
findings will inform the company’s marketing strategy. I will give the
Bellabeat leadership team your analysis and suggestions for high-level
marketing strategies.

\##Ask

Sršen invites me to evaluate smart device usage statistics to understand
non-Bellabeat smart device usage. She then wants you to use these
findings to present one Bellabeat product. Analyze these:

1.  Smart device utilization trends?
2.  How can these tendencies affect Bellabeat customers?
3.  How can these developments affect Bellabeat’s marketing?

Deliverables:

1.  A concise business task summary
2.  The data sources used
3.  Data cleaning and modification documentation
4.  Analysis Summary
5.  Visualizations and findings
6.  Your top high-level content recommendations from the analysis

\##Prepare

Kaggle dataset Mobius Fitbit Fitness Tracker Data.

\###Access and Privacy This dataset is licensed under CC0 1.0 Universal
Public Domain Dedication. So, this person has given the work to the
public domain by giving up all copyright rights worldwide, including
rights that are related or close by, to the extent that the law allows.

\###Dataset Information Between 03.12.2016 and 05.12.2016, Amazon
Mechanical Turk respondents created this dataset. Thirty eligible Fitbit
users agreed to provide personal tracker data, including minute-level
physical activity, heart rate, and sleep monitoring. The output varies
according to Fitbit tracker model and user tracking choices.

\###Is the data organized?

This dataset has 18.csv files—fifteen long and three wide format. This
dataset also includes weight logs, calories, sleep records, heart rate,
steps, and metabolic equivalent of tasks (METs)

\###Does the data ROCCC?

The word ROCCC is an acronym.

- Reliable
- Original
- Comprehensive
- Current
- Cited

FitBit Fitness Tracker Data (CC0: Public Domain, dataset available
through Mobius): Thirty Fitbit customers’ fitness trackers are in this
Kaggle data collection. Thirty Fitbit users who were eligible agreed to
send in their tracker data, which included minute-by-minute measurements
of physical activity, heart rate, and sleep. Users’ habits can be
examined using daily activity, step, and heart rate data. The data is
reliable and original. The data is comprehensive because it contains
many data types and variables. This dataset is not current, because
we’re in 2022, so it’s not.It’s not cited, either. This dataset is okay
and not good at the same time. We’ll work with it.
